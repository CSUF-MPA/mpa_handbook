Introduction

Mission

The mission of the MPA program at CSUF is to prepare our students for a career in public service by providing a core set of competencies anchored on the foundations of scholarly research and professional practice.

NASPAA Accreditation

Our MPA program is accredited by the Network of Schools of Public Policy, Affairs and Administration (NASPAA) since 1989.

Core Competencies:

  • Lead and manage in public governance
  • Participate in and contribute to the policy process
  • Analyze, synthesize, think critically, solve problems and make decisions
  • Articulate and apply a public service perspective
  • Communicate and interact productively with a diverse and changing workforce and citizenry

Capstone & Comprehensive Exam

Overview

The MPA Comprehensive Examination is administered as part of POSC 521 (Capstone Seminar in Public Administration Theory). Students must successfully complete both components of the comprehensive exam to graduate from the MPA program.

Comprehensive Exam Components

General Theory Essay

Tests knowledge of core public administration theories and concepts covered in the MPA program.

  • Take-home format
  • One week to complete
  • Based on core public administration theories and readings

Exam Process

Initial Attempt

  • Administered during POSC 521
  • Graded as Pass/Fail
  • Both components must be passed

Retake Process

  • First Retake: During finals week of the same semester
  • Second Retake (if necessary): Oral examination
    • Student receives an Incomplete in POSC 521
    • Final opportunity to pass the comprehensive exam
    • Must be completed within department guidelines

Prerequisites

  • Must be in final six units of graduate work
  • All core courses must be completed
  • All concentration courses must be completed

Frequently Asked Questions

Admissions

The minimum requirements include:

  • 2.75 GPA in undergraduate degree
  • Completed prerequisites
  • Statement of purpose
  • Two letters of recommendation
  • Current resume

Application deadlines are:

  • Fall Admission: April 30
  • Spring Admission: November 1

Program Requirements

The MPA program requires 36 units total:

  • 21 units of core courses
  • 9 units in your chosen concentration
  • 6 units of electives

Three prerequisites are required:

  • American Government or U.S. History
  • Statistics or Research Methods
  • Economics

These must be completed with a grade of "C" or better.

Course Planning

The timeline varies based on your course load:

  • Full-time (9 units/semester): 2 years
  • Part-time (6 units/semester): 3 years
  • Maximum time allowed: 5 years

Classes are scheduled with working professionals in mind:

  • Evening classes (7:00-9:45 PM)
  • Monday through Thursday
  • Mix of in-person and online formats

Graduation

To graduate, you must:

  • Complete all required courses (36 units)
  • Maintain a minimum 3.0 GPA
  • Pass the comprehensive exam
  • Complete the graduation check process

The comprehensive exam consists of the following:

  • One General Theory Essay Questions
  • Taken as a component of POSC 521: MPA Capstone
  • Covers general public administration theory and literature
  • A retake is allowed if necessary.
